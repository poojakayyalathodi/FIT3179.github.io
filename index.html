Plastic Pollution

Plastic, a dangerous manmade invention, once considered a blessing now becoming the reason for world-wide destruction.From, the pens we write with to the device that you are using to view this infographic, most likely has elements of plastic embedded to it. This visualisation gives us a good idea about the history of plastic and uses it to predict the future of plastic pollution & its impact on the world.


Plastic Waste Generated per Person (2010)

This world map shows us the per capita rate of plastic waste generation,measured in kilograms per person per day.

Daily per capita plastic waste generated some of the across highest countries - United States, Germany, Guyana is more than ten times that of the lowest countries - India, Bangladesh and Mozambique.

Find out the daily per capita plastic waste generated per person in your country by hovering over on the map.

Source: http://science.sciencemag.org/content/347/6223/768/

Factors determining plastic waste production

The chart on the right shows us plastic waste generated per person versus Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita. GDP per capita is an indicator used to make cross-country comparisons of average living standards and well-being.

Here, a pattern to be noticed is that plastic waste generation tends to increase as we get richer although there are variations across countries at all levels of development. Population has no influence over plastic waste production.

Source: http://science.sciencemag.org/content/347/6223/768/




Plastic Disposal Methods
(1950 - 2015)

This normalised area chart shows the evolution of different disposal methods used over the years. Hover over the graph to see the split between the % of discarded,incinerated and recycled plastic for each year.

There has been a steady improvement over the years in the way plastic is disposed with a stable decrease in discarded on average by 14% every ten years from 1990.From 1980 for inceration and 1990 for recycling, rates increased on average by 0.7% per year.

Scroll down to read more on the future of plastic waste disposal.

Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-019-49413-5l




Extrapolation of Change in Plastic Disposal Methods used

From the chart above, we concluded that incineration and recycling plastic has been gaining popularity over the years. Meaning that discardation of plastic will soon become part of the past.

This line chart shows the extrapolation of the historical trends displayed above to predict what methods are going to be used in the future, and as can be seen incineration rates would rise to 50% and recycling to 44% by 2050.

Source: http://advances.sciencemag.org/content/3/7/e1700782.full




Microplastics in Surface Ocean based on emission trends
(1950-2050)

Microplastics are bouyant plastic materials that are smaller than 0.5 cm in diameter.The chart on the right shows the prediction from 2019 to 2050 for differenent emission scenarios.

There is a possiblity of reducing the amount of microplastics going into oceans by about 200% if we stop emissions by 2020, compared to continuing to emit at a stagnant rate and growth of emission rate.

Macroplastics in Surface Ocean based on emission trends
(1950-2050)

Macroplastics are bouyant plastic materials that are greater than 0.5 cm in diameter. The chart on the right shows the prediction from 2019 to 2050 for differenent emission scenarios.

Unlike, microplastics amount of macroplastics in the oceans reduces by 54% , compared to the amount of macroplastics in the ocean in 2020.

Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-019-49413-5
